Hormone Replacement Therapy

HRT:
 

The physicians of Boca Raton Ob/Gyn Specialists are experts in evaluating for and treating hormone deficiencies in women. They are very involved in educating their patients so that they may together as a team, discern the issues at hand and formulate a plan that is most suitable for the patient.
 

Many symptoms such as decreased libido, low energy level, hot flashes or flushes, night sweats and insomnia may be alleviated. Vaginal atrophy resulting from estrogen deficiency and leading to vulva-vaginal thinning and dryness and pain, as well as associated bladder irritability, weakness and infection, may be remedied as well.

 
Treatment modalities may include administering estrogen, testosterone, and progesterone, and there are numerous alternative choices  available. We prescribe natural, bio-identical, and FDA approved hormonal replacement therapies. These may be available as vaginal creams and tablets; topical lotions, creams, gels and patches; oral tablets and chewables and dissolving substances, aerosols, injections and implants.  


Not everyone may be a suitable candidate for each treatment and as there are sometimes side effects or risks that may outweigh the benefit being targeted. Taking care in evaluating each patient individually for what their needs may be and assessing for a potential hormonal deficiency, while keeping the patient as a whole in mind, is prudent. Astute assessment requires attentive listening to the patients' history and family history, concerns and symptoms. Performing relevant physical examination and appropriate laboratory studies and perhaps mammography pelvic ultrasound and bone densitometry may serve crucial as well. 


If hormone replacement therapy is to be initiated then periodic monitoring for potential side effects and adverse events needs to be part of the follow up plan . Maintaining periodic blood pressure monitoring, pelvic ultrasound periodically depending on the patient, regular visits to assess for any changes in medications, and surveillance of symptoms should be practiced. in the event that a major surgery is to be planned or another event that may require immobilization for an extended time is anticipated, the hormone replacement therapy should be discontinued several weeks ahead of time. This serves to reduce the risk of deep venous thrombosis or embolism. The therapy usually may be restarted after being fully ambulatory .
